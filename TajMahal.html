Taj Mahal

A Symbol of Love and Architectural Beauty

The Taj Mahal in Agra, India – one of the most iconic monuments in the world.

🏛️About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the most famous historical monuments in the world and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. It was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The monument is admired globally for its beauty and perfect architectural symmetry.

Located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, on the banks of the Yamuna River, the Taj Mahal reflects a blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. Its white marble structure, detailed carvings, and intricate inlay work make it one of the most beautiful buildings ever created.

Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653. More than 20,000 artisans and craftsmen contributed to the construction of this grand monument.

Today the Taj Mahal is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

📌Quick Information

  • Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Built By: Shah Jahan
  • Construction Period: 1632 – 1653
  • Architectural Style: Mughal Architecture
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site

🏗️Architecture and Design

The Taj Mahal is built from white marble that changes color depending on the time of day and lighting conditions. The monument stands on a large platform and is surrounded by four elegant minarets designed to tilt slightly outward.

The central dome rises about 73 meters high and is surrounded by smaller domes and decorative carvings made with precious stones.

📜Historical Timeline

  • 1632 – Construction begins.
  • 1648 – Main mausoleum completed.
  • 1653 – Entire complex finished.
  • 1983 – UNESCO World Heritage recognition.

ℹ️Visitor Information

The Taj Mahal is open to visitors throughout the year except on Fridays. Millions of tourists visit the monument every year to admire its beauty and historical significance.

  • Opening Hours: Sunrise to Sunset
  • Closed Day: Friday
  • Best Time to Visit: October – March

🌏Cultural Importance

The Taj Mahal is not only an architectural masterpiece but also a symbol of eternal love. It has become one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world and represents India's cultural and artistic heritage.

✨Interesting Facts

Built using white marble from Makrana in Rajasthan.
More than 20,000 artisans worked on the monument.
Construction took around 22 years to complete.
The Taj Mahal changes color depending on sunlight and moonlight.
The four surrounding minarets lean slightly outward for safety.

💬Famous Quote

"The Taj Mahal rises above the banks of the river like a solitary tear suspended on the cheek of time." — Rabindranath Tagore

🔍Explore More

Why is the Taj Mahal considered an architectural masterpiece?

It combines Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles with perfect symmetry and intricate marble craftsmanship.

Why does the Taj Mahal change color?

The marble reflects sunlight and moonlight differently throughout the day.

Why do the minarets lean slightly outward?

The design protects the main structure during earthquakes.
